Market Timing
The quantum threat is no longer theoretical.
Google, IBM, and nation-state actors are advancing quantum hardware on a timeline that makes today's blockchain infrastructure genuinely fragile. The window to build quantum-safe infrastructure at the base layer is narrowing.
01 — THREAT

$2.5T+ in on-chain assets at risk

Every blockchain using ECDSA or Ed25519 — including Bitcoin and Ethereum — is vulnerable to a sufficiently powerful quantum computer. The attack surface is not the algorithm layer. It's the key layer.

02 — TIMING

NIST finalized post-quantum standards in 2024

FIPS 204 (ML-DSA) and FIPS 205 (SPHINCS+) are now finalized federal standards. Quantar implements both natively — not as an upgrade path, but as the only signature scheme that has ever existed on the chain.

03 — MOAT

Genesis-level enforcement is irreplaceable

Existing chains cannot migrate to post-quantum cryptography without breaking backward compatibility. Quantar has no legacy debt. The security property cannot be replicated by retrofitting.
