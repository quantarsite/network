The Quantum Threat Is Global. So Is Our Response.

Quantum computing is no longer a distant possibility — it is an approaching reality. Major technology companies including Google, IBM, and Microsoft are racing toward quantum supremacy, with functional quantum computers expected by 2030. When they arrive, the cryptographic foundations that protect every blockchain, every cryptocurrency wallet, and every digital transaction will become vulnerable.

The numbers are staggering: $2.5 trillion in digital assets currently rely on cryptographic algorithms — ECDSA and RSA — that quantum computers will be able to break. Bitcoin, Ethereum, and every major blockchain face the same existential threat. This is not a problem that can be fixed with a simple software update. It requires a fundamentally new approach to blockchain architecture.

That is exactly what Quantar Network is building.

The Technology Behind Quantar

Quantar is not a fork of an existing blockchain, and it is not a layer built on top of another chain. It is a completely new Layer-1 blockchain designed from the genesis block with post-quantum cryptography.

We use three NIST-standardized algorithms that have been rigorously tested and approved by the United States National Institute of Standards and Technology:

CRYSTALS-Dilithium-5 for digital signatures — replacing the vulnerable ECDSA used by Bitcoin and Ethereum. Kyber-3 for key encapsulation — ensuring secure key exchange that quantum computers cannot intercept. And SPHINCS+ for hash-based signatures — providing an additional layer of security based on different mathematical foundations.

Our 128-shard architecture targets over 100,000 transactions per second, combining quantum security with the performance needed for real-world adoption.
