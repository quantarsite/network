The Problem
Every major blockchain runs on cryptography quantum computers will break.

Bitcoin, Ethereum, and every Layer-1 blockchain in production today rely on ECDSA or EdDSA — classical elliptic-curve signatures that are vulnerable to Shor's algorithm. When large-scale quantum computers arrive, these signatures become forgeable. $2.5 trillion in digital assets are at risk.

Retrofitting post-quantum cryptography onto existing chains means hard forks, coexistence of classical and quantum-safe credentials, ambiguous verification paths, and migration debt that accumulates across every account, wallet, and smart contract.

The Solution
Build it right from block one.

Quantar Network enforces HYDRA-X — a dual post-quantum signature protocol — at the consensus layer from genesis. Every transaction, every block, every validator attestation carries two structurally independent post-quantum signatures bound to a shared transcript. No classical credentials ever exist in the system.

HYDRA-X Components

Component Standard Hardness Role
ML-DSA-87 FIPS 204 NIST L5 Module-LWE / Module-SIS Primary — compact, fast
SLH-DSA-SHAKE-256f FIPS 205 NIST L5 Hash function security Secondary — conservative, minimal assumptions

A breakthrough against lattice cryptography doesn't affect the hash-based component. A breakthrough against hash-based schemes doesn't affect the lattice component. The AND-composition guarantees the composed scheme is at least as strong as the stronger of the two.
